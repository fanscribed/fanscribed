Fanscribed Transcription Engine (FTE)

Fanscribed Transcription Engine (FTE) is a unique way to manage all aspects of creating an accurate written transcript of recorded speech using crowdsourced human workers.

FTE differs from wiki-style crowdsourced transcription (as used by the Classic Fanscribed open source project):

  • A quality-control process leads to very accurate transcripts.
  • Task isolation allows casual volunteers to help without requiring deep knowledge (such as speaker names).

FTE differs from traditional single-person transcription:

  • Tedium is relieved by time-boxing each transcription task, and varying the types of work.
  • The quality-control process can be spread throughout a group, leading to higher accuracy.

How FTE Works

The transcript is first split into segments, usually 10 seconds in length. Then, the transcript goes through five stages, each broken into many individual tasks:

  1. Transcribe: In each segment, every spoken sentence or sentence fragment is transcribed to text.
  2. Stitch: At the edges of each fragment, sentence fragments are bound together into complete sentences.
  3. Boundary: The precise time code for each sentence is found.
  4. Clean: Capitalization, punctuation, repetition, and other errors are corrected.
  5. Speaker: The speaker of each sentence is identified.

After each task, another person is given that task to review it, and make any needed changes.

Once all stages are completed, you have an accurate, time-coded transcript!
